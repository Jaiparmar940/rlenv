## The one thing that changed while doing this

The results table you had was **not a valid comparison**, and the audit caught it.
Its 36 frontier episodes ran under the *previous* uncoached prompt; only haiku's 9
ran under the current one (which added the `read_pid` "reads the vehicle in its
current state" paragraph). Rows under different prompts are not a benchmark.

The matrix was rerun once, all 5 models, one prompt revision. **The finding
survived and the numbers moved only slightly:**

| | old (mixed prompts) | new (one prompt) |
|---|---|---|
| frontier mean | 93.7 | **94.6** |
| haiku-4-5 | 39.8 | **47.9** |
| separation | 56.5 | **50.0** |

Two substantive corrections came out of the new transcripts:

- **The deployment-tier failure mode was mis-characterized.** It is not primarily
  "condemns the alternator after misreading `alt_output`" (that was 1/9, and the
  prompt fix largely addressed it). It is a **parts cannon**: across the six
  ground-fault episodes haiku-4-5 replaced **eleven innocent parts** — battery 5x,
  alternator 4x, starter motor 2x — on a car whose fault is a $25 ground strap. In one
  episode it inspected the strap, wrote *"Aha! The ground strap end is greenish"*,
  and still scored 11.7, because it had already swapped two innocent parts and
  never verified a fix. Red-herring scenario: **0/3 verified fixes.**
- **A grader-design note worth keeping.** `measured-first` is 3/3 for those
  episodes — the model always measures once, so the guessing cap never fires. The
  only rule that catches *measure-once-then-shotgun* is wrong-parts-debit-the-total.
  Without it these episodes would have scored as disciplined.

The coached A/B was also re-measured under the current prompt pair (the 57.4 /
38.3 numbers were stale): **coached 61.5 vs uncoached 47.9, a 13.6-point coaching
effect**, verified-fix 8/9 → 5/9. Coaching does not rescue the model — the red
herring still scores 20.9 coached.

## Physics flag that touched `main` — RESOLVED 2026-07-12

Building the compound scenario forced a series-circuit solve of the model's own
constants, and `GROUND_CURRENT_RECOVERY = 0.6` did not close against it (~11.45 V
at the battery under cranking in `medium_corroded_ground` vs ~10.40 V from the
series arithmetic). **Jaivir ruled: the model must be series-consistent, in both
tiers.** The constant is now derived in `propagation.py` (recovery/drop =
healthy_sag/OCV = 2.8/12.6 ≈ 0.222 — exact for any strap resistance in a linear
series circuit). Innocent ground-scenario batteries now crank at ~10.41/10.47 V
(still a passing load test; ground drops and resting readings unchanged), the
compound weak battery at ~8.59 V (still fails). All 104 tests, sanity_check, and
check_determinism green. **Consequence: the published results table was generated
under the old calibration and needs a rerun before publish** — models saw battery
readings ~1 V higher than the environment now produces.

Also worth knowing before you play the hard tier: the intermittent ECU scenario is a
**0.0 wall** for haiku-4-5 (0/3, mean 0.0) — it never suspects the ECU, swaps a part,
catches a lucky start (the fault manifests on ~65% of cranks) and declares victory.
The open question on that branch is whether that scenario is *discriminating* or
merely *brutal*.
